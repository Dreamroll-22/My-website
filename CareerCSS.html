ICT Career Paths For Computer System Servicing

1. Computer Technician

  • Difficulty Level: ★★★☆☆ (Moderate)
  • Salary Range (Philippines): ₱10,000 – ₱20,000 per month (Entry-level)
  • Courses to Take: CSS NCII, Computer Hardware Servicing, Basic Networking
  • Job Description: Repairs and maintains computers, upgrades hardware, and troubleshoots system issues.
  • Pros: High demand, opportunities for freelancing.
  • Cons: Requires patience in troubleshooting, exposure to dust and electrical hazards.

2. IT Support Specialist

  • Difficulty Level: ★★★☆☆ (Moderate)
  • Salary Range (Philippines): ₱18,000 – ₱30,000 per month
  • Courses to Take: CSS NCII, IT Support, Basic Networking, Customer Support Training
  • Job Description: Provides technical support to customers/employees by troubleshooting software and hardware issues.
  • Pros: Stable job in many industries.
  • Cons: Can be stressful due to customer complaints.

3. Network Technician

  • Difficulty Level: ★★★★☆ (Challenging)
  • Salary Range (Philippines): ₱20,000 – ₱35,000 per month
  • Courses to Take: CSS NCII, Networking NCII/NCIII, Cisco Networking, IT Essentials
  • Job Description: Installs and maintains network infrastructure, routers, and modems.
  • Pros: High-paying with career growth.
  • Cons: Requires fieldwork, must troubleshoot under pressure.

4. System Administrator

  • Difficulty Level: ★★★★☆ (Challenging)
  • Salary Range (Philippines): ₱25,000 – ₱50,000 per month
  • Courses to Take: IT or Computer Science, System Administration, Linux, Windows Server Management
  • Job Description: Manages a company’s IT infrastructure, ensuring systems run smoothly.
  • Pros: High salary, in-demand skills.
  • Cons: Requires continuous learning and handling system failures.

5. Hardware Assembler

  • Difficulty Level: ★★☆☆☆ (Easy to Moderate)
  • Salary Range (Philippines): ₱12,000 – ₱18,000 per month
  • Courses to Take: CSS NCII, PC Assembly, Hardware Troubleshooting
  • Job Description: Assembles computers and other electronic devices.
  • Pros: No advanced IT knowledge needed.
  • Cons: Low salary, repetitive work.

6. Technical Support Representative (TSR) – Call Center IT Support

  • Difficulty Level: ★★★☆☆ (Moderate)
  • Salary Range (Philippines): ₱18,000 – ₱40,000 per month
  • Courses to Take: CSS NCII, Customer Service Training, IT Fundamentals
  • Job Description: Provides remote troubleshooting for IT issues in a call center environment.
  • Pros: High salary, good benefits.
  • Cons: Night shifts, stressful calls from customers.

7. Help Desk Support

  • Difficulty Level: ★★★☆☆ (Moderate)
  • Salary Range (Philippines): ₱18,000 – ₱35,000 per month
  • Courses to Take: IT or CSS NCII, Customer Service, Networking
  • Job Description: Assists employees/customers with technical issues via phone or in person.
  • Pros: Good salary, opportunity to advance.
  • Cons: Requires patience and strong problem-solving skills.

8. Network Administrator

  • Difficulty Level: ★★★★☆ (Challenging)
  • Salary Range (Philippines): ₱30,000 – ₱60,000 per month
  • Courses to Take: IT, Networking, Cybersecurity, Cisco Certification (CCNA)
  • Job Description: Manages and secures company networks.
  • Pros: High demand, excellent salary.
  • Cons: Requires advanced knowledge and troubleshooting skills.

9. Cybersecurity Analyst (Entry-Level)

  • Difficulty Level: ★★★★★ (Very Challenging)
  • Salary Range (Philippines): ₱40,000 – ₱80,000 per month
  • Courses to Take: IT, Cybersecurity, Ethical Hacking, CompTIA Security+
  • Job Description: Protects data and networks from cyber threats.
  • Pros: High salary, job security.
  • Cons: Requires continuous learning and certification.

10. Printer and Peripheral Technician

  • Difficulty Level: ★★☆☆☆ (Easy to Moderate)
  • Salary Range (Philippines): ₱10,000 – ₱18,000 per month
  • Courses to Take: CSS NCII, Printer Repair Training
  • Job Description: Repairs printers, scanners, and other devices.
  • Pros: Easy to learn.
  • Cons: Low salary, few job openings.

11. CCTV Technician

  • Difficulty Level: ★★★☆☆ (Moderate)
  • Salary Range (Philippines): ₱12,000 – ₱25,000 per month
  • Courses to Take: CSS NCII, CCTV Installation & Maintenance
  • Job Description: Installs and repairs security camera systems.
  • Pros: Growing demand in businesses.
  • Cons: Requires fieldwork and electrical knowledge.

12. Freelance Computer Technician

  • Difficulty Level: ★★★☆☆ (Moderate)
  • Salary Range: ₱5,000 – ₱50,000 per month (Depends on clients)
  • Courses to Take: CSS NCII, PC Troubleshooting
  • Job Description: Offers repair and IT services independently.
  • Pros: Be your own boss, flexible income.
  • Cons: Income is unstable.

13. Computer Repair Shop Owner

  • Difficulty Level: ★★★★☆ (Challenging)
  • Salary Range: ₱20,000 – ₱100,000+ per month (Depends on business)
  • Courses to Take: CSS NCII, Business Management
  • Job Description: Runs a shop that offers computer repair and services.
  • Pros: High earning potential.
  • Cons: Requires capital and business skills.

14. Custom PC Builder

  • Difficulty Level: ★★★☆☆ (Moderate)
  • Salary Range: ₱10,000 – ₱50,000 per month (Depends on orders)
  • Courses to Take: CSS NCII, PC Assembly, Hardware Optimization
  • Job Description: Builds high-performance PCs for gaming or work.
  • Pros: Passion-based job, high profit per build.
  • Cons: Needs marketing and technical knowledge.

15. Smartphone Repair Technician

  • Difficulty Level: ★★★☆☆ (Moderate)
  • Salary Range: ₱10,000 – ₱25,000 per month
  • Courses to Take: Mobile Phone Repair Training
  • Job Description: Repairs smartphones and tablets.
  • Pros: High demand.
  • Cons: Requires delicate skills.
